The College Radio Problem

Every college station deals with the same challenges:

  • Dead air — DJs don't show up, shifts go unfilled, overnight has no coverage
  • Budget constraints — professional radio software costs $5,000-15,000. Student activity fees don't cover that.
  • Staff turnover — every year you lose seniors and train freshmen. Nothing is consistent.
  • Technical complexity — most radio software requires someone who knows audio engineering, networking, and server administration
  • Streaming costs — hosting a separate streaming server adds monthly expense

What College Radio Actually Needs

After talking to station managers, student DJs, and faculty advisors, the wish list is always the same:

  1. Automatic operation — the station runs 24/7 whether someone's there or not
  2. Easy enough for any student — no training manual, no engineering degree
  3. Affordable — under $100/month, ideally under $50
  4. Live DJ capability — when students DO show up, they can go live easily
  5. Something between songs — not just song-song-song, but actual DJ presence
